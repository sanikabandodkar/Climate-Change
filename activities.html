Human Activities

Human activities are the main cause of climate change, and include:

  • Burning fossil fuels: Burning coal, oil, and gas for electricity and heat releases carbon dioxide and nitrous oxide into the atmosphere
  • Deforestation: Cutting down forests releases carbon dioxide into the atmosphere, as trees absorb carbon dioxide to regulate the climate
  • Livestock farming: Cows and sheep produce methane when they digest food
  • Fertilizers: Nitrogen-containing fertilizers release nitrous oxide emissions
  • Fluorinated gases: Equipment and products that use these gases emit emissions that are 23,000 times more warming than carbon dioxide
  • Oil and gas development: Drilling, fracking, transporting, and refining oil and gas
  • Buildings: Heating, cooling, cooking, and running appliances
