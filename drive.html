Drive Less

Driving less can have a significant impact on reducing greenhouse gas emissions and mitigating climate change. Here's how individuals can help by driving less:

  • Use Alternative Modes of Transportation: Opt for walking, cycling, or using public transportation whenever possible. These modes of transportation produce fewer emissions compared to driving, especially if the public transit system is powered by clean energy sources
  • Carpooling and Ridesharing: Share rides with friends, family, or coworkers to reduce the number of vehicles on the road and lower emissions per passenger. Consider using ridesharing services or carpooling apps to connect with others traveling in the same direction.
  • Telecommuting and Remote Work: If feasible, work from home or telecommute to reduce the need for daily commuting. Remote work not only reduces emissions from transportation but also saves time and resources associated with commuting.
  • Combine Errands: Plan your trips efficiently by combining multiple errands into one outing. This reduces the number of separate trips and minimizes fuel consumption and emissions.
  • Drive Efficiently: When driving is necessary, practice fuel-efficient driving habits to reduce fuel consumption and emissions. This includes maintaining a steady speed, avoiding sudden acceleration and braking, and keeping your vehicle well-maintained.
  • Choose Fuel-Efficient Vehicles: If purchasing a new vehicle, choose one with high fuel efficiency or consider electric or hybrid options. Electric vehicles (EVs) produce zero tailpipe emissions and are increasingly accessible as charging infrastructure expands.
  • Reduce Idling: Avoid idling your vehicle unnecessarily, as idling consumes fuel and emits pollutants. Turn off your engine when parked or waiting for extended periods, such as at drive-throughs or while waiting to pick someone up.
